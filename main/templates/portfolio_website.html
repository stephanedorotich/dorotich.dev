In 2021, I had the opportunity to take CPSC 399, a course in Self-directed study.

At the time, I hadn't the faintest idea how the internet actually worked, nor how website's were made to look the way they do.

I spent the semester investigating the basics of browsers and the three languages that make up the bulk of web-browsing traffic: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

At the end of the semester, I decided to put my new-found knowledge to use and build a website to advertise my tutoring business. The pandemic had affected my client base and so I needed a way to rebuild it.

Without any understanding of certificates or encryption, I discovered that GitHub Pages was my easiest way to providing a secure website. My efforts paid off and Fall 2021 was my busiest semester of tutoring ever.
