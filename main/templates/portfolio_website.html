In 2021, I had the opportunity to take CPSC 399, a course in self-directed study in computer science.

At the time, I did not understand how the internet worked (networking: packets, protocols, etc.), nor how websites were made to look the way they do.

I spent the semester investigating the basic functionality of browsers and how HTML, CSS, and JavaScript work together to present the interactive face of the internet.

At the end of the semester, I put my new-found knowledge to use and built a website to promote my tutoring business, which had shrunk over the course of the pandemic and was in need of some advertising.

Because I still didn't understand the nature of https (encryption and certificates) I found that GitHub Pages was an accessible means of hosting a secure website. After launching the website and promoting it with Google and Facebook, I had the busiest semester of tutoring to date in Fall 2021.
